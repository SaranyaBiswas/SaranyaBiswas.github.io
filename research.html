Research Interests

Stochastic Dynamical Systems

Investigating noise-induced phenomena in nonlinear systems, including stochastic attractors, noise-driven transitions, and basin stability analysis of complex systems.

Nonlinear Oscillations

Studying limit cycles, bifurcations, intermittency, and nonlinear response behavior in deterministic and stochastic oscillatory systems.

Computational Methods

Developing numerical simulations, stochastic integrators, and computational algorithms to characterize noisy mechanical systems and multiscale dynamical processes.

Climate Dynamics and Stochastic Modelling

Exploring nonlinear mechanisms behind climate variability, tipping points, regime shifts, and noise-induced transitions using stochastic dynamical systems and global stability frameworks.

System Identification from Time Series

Working on data-driven discovery of governing equations using approaches such as SINDy, sparse regression, and operator learning. Focus areas include identifying hidden nonlinear structures from noisy time series and linking data to reduced-order models.

Dynamics of Beams, Impacts, and Nonlinear Mechanical Systems

Analyzing nonlinear vibrational behavior in structural and mechanical systems such as beams, impact oscillators, and multi-degree-of-freedom systems. Includes studying localization, intermittency, and global stability under stochastic excitations.
