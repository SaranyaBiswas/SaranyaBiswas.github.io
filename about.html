About Me

I am a researcher specializing in nonlinear stochastic dynamics, focusing on noisy dynamical systems, stochastic stability, and uncertainty quantification. My research blends numerical simulation, probability theory, and analytical modelling to better understand complex dynamical behaviour under random forcing.

I completed my MS–PhD in Applied Mechanics at IIT Madras, working on the characterization of stochastic attractors and noise-driven transitions in nonlinear systems. Following this, I worked as a Project Engineer at IIT Kanpur under Prof. Anindya Chatterjee, exploring topics in survivability, noise-induced intermittency, and nonlinear vibrations.

I have experience in teaching undergraduate and postgraduate engineering courses, and I have presented my research at international conferences including PND (IIT Madras), ICoEV (Aberdeen), and CNSD (IIT Kanpur).

Research Themes

  • Stochastic differential equations
  • Random vibration and uncertainty propagation
  • Basin stability and survivability
  • Noise-induced bifurcations
  • Nonlinear oscillations under random forcing
