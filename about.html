About Me

I am Saranya Biswas, a researcher in nonlinear and stochastic dynamical systems. I completed my B.E. in Mechanical Engineering from Jadavpur University, followed by an MS–PhD in Applied Mechanics at IIT Madras, where my work focused on noise-induced phenomena, stochastic attractors, and stability analysis of nonlinear systems.

I subsequently worked as a Project Engineer at IIT Kanpur, where I continued to develop computational tools for noisy dynamical systems, nonlinear oscillators, and mechanical models involving impacts and intermittent responses. I later served briefly as a Visiting Assistant Professor in Kolkata before transitioning full-time into independent research.

My broader research interests span stochastic bifurcations, climate-related dynamics, nonlinear vibrations, and data-driven system identification.

I am currently expanding my computational toolkit by learning modern machine-learning approaches — including scikit-learn, sparse model discovery, and data-driven modeling techniques for nonlinear and stochastic systems.
